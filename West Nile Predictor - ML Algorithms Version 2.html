Edit: this was figured out after everything else was coded. Notice how the mean for train is 5%. This means that 5% of the time the ML algorithm is taught to say 'yes' or '1' to West Nile Virus and 95% of the time is taught to say 'no' or '0'. This is why we are getting 95% accuracy for all classification ML. It's not predicting the virus, it's telling us how accurate it can say no or 0.

This is happening because of unbalanced data. Our minority, 1, is under represented while our majority, 0, is over representented. What needs to happen is a resampling of random data that gives us an equal representation of our majority and minority.
